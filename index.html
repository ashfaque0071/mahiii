The 20th of September

20 · 09 · 2022

The day you became inseparable from me. And every second since —

— I have loved you. Even in the moments I failed you, I never once stopped. 🤍

Written on the 8th of July

Mahi,

I don't know if my words can reach the place inside you that I have hurt. I don't know if this message will make anything better. But I still want to speak honestly, because there are things I should have understood earlier, said earlier, and shown earlier.

I don't have fancy words. I only have a heart that has loved you in ways I often failed to express properly. I love your smile, your voice, your eyes, your laugh, the way you talk, the way you get excited over small things, the way you feel deeply, the way you care, the way you become soft even when the world has been hard on you. I love the tiny details of you that maybe you don't even notice about yourself. Not just the easy parts. Not just the happy version of you. I love you as a whole person.

But I also know that loving you was never supposed to mean hurting you. And that is where I failed.

I know I have hurt you through my immaturity, my panic, my poor decisions, my misunderstandings, my inability to listen properly, and my habit of wanting everything to become normal before your pain was actually healed. I know I made you feel unsafe. I know I made you feel unheard. I know I made you feel like your emotions were too much for me — when actually your emotions deserved patience, gentleness, and respect.

You deserved a partner who could sit with your pain without defending himself. You deserved someone who could understand your words, your silence, your mood, your small expectations, your excitement, your disappointment, and your tiredness. You deserved someone who would not make you explain the same hurt again and again just to be understood.

I am sorry that I was not that person when you needed me to be.

I am sorry for the times I acted like a child when you needed maturity from me. For the times I reacted from fear instead of understanding. For the times I made my panic bigger than your pain. For the times I begged for forgiveness but did not give you enough proof of change. For the times I wanted you to stay, but did not make staying feel safe for you.

I know I cannot simply say "forget everything" and expect you to become okay. Pain does not disappear just because someone says sorry. Trust does not come back just because love is still there. Respect, safety, and peace need to be rebuilt through actions, not emotional words.

And I know I damaged more than just moments. I damaged memories that should have been beautiful. You always wanted me to get into BUET. You were excited for me. You wanted to see me grow, succeed, and become something better. That journey should have been something I shared with you with happiness — my classes, my new routine, my small daily changes should have naturally included you. Because of my wrong handling, I failed to share those parts of my life with you. I made you feel outside of a life where you should have felt important.

And now I understand why it hurts you. The days where you should have felt proud, excited, and close to me became connected with pain and distance. I cannot bring those days back. But I can say clearly: I am sorry that my journey, which you once cared about so much, became a source of hurt for you because of me.

I also know your own life has pressure — your admission exam, your studies, your family, your future, your peace. I should have made your life lighter, not heavier. I should have protected your peace, not added to it. I do not want you to damage your future because of me. I want you to study, to grow, to succeed, to become everything you are capable of becoming. If I truly love you, I have to respect your future, not pull you away from it because of my insecurity.

I am not writing this to make you feel guilty. Not to force you to come back. Not to say "look, I am sad, so you have to choose me." I do not want to love you in a way that traps you. I want to love you in a way that finally gives you safety.

I still want a future with you, Mahi. I cannot lie about that. I still imagine a life where we heal properly, where we talk calmly, where we understand each other, where we build something peaceful instead of painful — the ordinary mornings, the small stories, the silly fights that end softly, the growing together. I still want to marry you someday. I still want a forever with you.

But I also understand that I cannot demand forever from someone I have hurt. I cannot ask you to trust the future if I have not repaired the present. I want to become someone you can feel safe with again — someone who listens before defending, understands before reacting, and respects your boundaries even when my heart is scared. Someone whose presence gives you calm, not pressure.

I know you may not believe me now. My words have lost value because my actions did not support them enough. I cannot blame you for that fear — I gave you reasons to feel it. But I want you to know that I am finally seeing things differently. I am not only afraid of losing you now. I am afraid of remaining the kind of person who hurt the person he loved most. I do not want to be that person anymore. And I will be honest about one more thing: I miss you in a way I do not have words for, and you are on my mind every single day. My love for you is not small, and it is not passing — it lives quietly in everything I do. But I will carry that longing myself. I will never make it your burden, and I will never use it to pull you back before you feel truly ready.

I am sorry for the pain.

I am sorry for the pressure.

I am sorry for the broken promises.

I am sorry for the immaturity.

I am sorry for not listening.

I am sorry for making you feel alone even while I said I loved you.

I am sorry for turning your excitement into hurt.

I am sorry for not becoming the safe place you needed.

You are not wrong for being tired. You are not wrong for being distant. You are not wrong for not trusting me immediately. You are not wrong for needing time, space, or proof. I will not blame your pain when my behavior helped create it.

I love you deeply. But now I understand that love has to be shown through patience, respect, understanding, and changed behavior — not only through emotional words. So I am not asking you to forget. I am not asking you to become normal today. I am only asking you to know that I finally understand that I did not just hurt you once — I hurt your sense of safety with me. And that is something I have to take seriously.

If there is ever a way back to us, I want it to be slow, honest, respectful, and safe for you. Built on real change, not fear. On listening, not pressure. On peace, not begging.

You mattered to me then.

You matter to me now.

You will always matter to me.

Not because I want to own you. Not because I want to force you to stay. But because my heart truly loves you, and I finally understand that loving you means respecting your pain too.

Forever, I will regret hurting you. And from now on, if I get any place in your life again, I want to earn it through the kind of behavior you deserved from me all along.

Yours — and finally learning how to be ♥
